The Soul that Shines

Beneath the sky's most tender hue,
There blooms a world made bright by you.
With eyes of midnight, deep and kind,
You hold the stars within your mind.


Your hair, a river dark and wild,
Flows soft as dreams of a gentle child.
Each silken strand, each whispered sigh,
Brings heaven's calm down from the sky.


Samia — your name, a secret prayer,
A song that lingers in the air.
Your smile, a dawn the heart can see,
Unfolds the flowers inside of me.


In fields where quiet moments stay,
Your laugh becomes the break of day.
No finer silk, no brighter flame,
Than the sweet light that bears your name.


You are the hush of evening air,
The blush of roses unaware,
The gentle grace, the fearless art,
Of loving with a purest heart.
No artist's hand, no poet's pen,
Could ever dream you whole again.
For all of beauty bends its knee,
Before your soul, your mystery.


And if the world were stripped of light,
I'd find my way by your black eyes' night.
For Samia — you are all I see,
My breath, my dream, my destiny.
